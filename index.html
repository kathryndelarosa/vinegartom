About

I've smiled on camera exactly one time in a dim sum restaurant 

I'm a dramaturg, sound designer, writer, radio producer, front-end web developer, occasional actor, and wedding/funeral cellist.

I've worked with IU Theatre, University Players, Asolo Repertory Theatre, and the Kentucky Center Governor's School for the Arts.

I used to repair rental violins. I also executive produced American Student Radio, assistant produced The Quietus Hour, and contributed to programs at WFHB.

Born in Prestonsburg and raised in Paducah, my soul is in Kentucky, but my flesh prison is currently based in southern Indiana and Sarasota, Florida.

I'm really passionate about Smithsonian Folkways, UX's place in dramaturgy, and Paddington Bear. As a scholar, I'm interested in rural, queer and post-colonial identities and intersections of performance and mass media. I'm also into astrology and tarot, and any time rock musicians sing Brecht.
